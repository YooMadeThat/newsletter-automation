# Allen + Clarke — Newsletter Brand Kit & Style Guide
## Policy + Regulation

Authoritative style reference for the AI-assisted weekly newsletter
workflow. Loaded into the system prompts of the summarise and compose
agents. Applied consistently across all content components.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Name treatment:** *Allen + Clarke* — always with the `+` connector,
not "and" or "&".

**Public tagline:** *We give a damn.*

**Purpose:** *Allen + Clarke empowers clients and communities to
overcome the challenges facing society.*

**Service naming convention:** Practice areas use the `+` connector
(e.g. *Policy + Regulation*, *Strategy + Planning*, *Evaluation +
Research*). Retain this convention in all headings.

---

## 2. Brand Colours

| Role | Colour | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Slate charcoal | `#3D4855` | Headings, body text, primary UI |
| Accent | Sage green | `#C8D9A0` | Highlights, call-outs, section dividers |
| Background | White | `#FFFFFF` | Page background, negative space |

Charcoal dominant. Sage green sparingly for emphasis. Do not introduce
additional colours without explicit brand approval.

---

## 3. Typography

**Headings:** Modern geometric sans-serif (Inter, Poppins, or
Montserrat). Weight 600–700. Neutral letter spacing. No italics in
headings.

**Body:** Sans-serif digital. Word output: Calibri 11pt, line height
1.4.

**Emphasis:** Bold for key terms; italics sparingly for publication
names or quoted titles. Never underline except for hyperlinks.

---

## 4. Voice & Tone

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Expert but human. Confident, never cold. Write like a trusted senior adviser, not a press release. |
| **Perspective** | *We* for newsletter inserts (shared team voice). *I* only in signed blog posts. |
| **Spelling** | NZ/British English throughout. *Prioritise, organisation, colour, programme, analyse, recognise, labour.* |

### Five voice attributes

1. **Plain** — short-to-medium sentences, active voice, no bureaucratese.
2. **Confident** — states things directly, does not hedge without cause.
3. **Purposeful** — every sentence earns its place. No filler.
4. **Analytical** — evidence-led, data-anchored, implication-focused.
5. **Human** — warm, never cold. The work serves people.

---

## 5. Structure & Formatting

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| **Sentences** | Short to medium. Vary the rhythm — don't string 5 long sentences together. |
| **Paragraphs** | 2–4 sentences max. White space is content. |
| **Headings** | H3 subheadings. Descriptive and standalone — readable without the body text. |
| **Bold** | Key ideas inline only. Not decoration. One or two per paragraph, max. |

---

## 6. Data & Titles

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| **Data** | Use specific numbers and named methodologies. *"A 23% reduction"* beats *"significant decrease".* |
| **Titles** | Specific, number-driven, slightly unexpected. Earn the click — don't summarise. Max 12 words. |
| **"So what for us" line** | Named, low-pressure. Short note on how this connects to Allen + Clarke's work or a client situation. |

---

## 7. Audience Personas

### Debbie — Senior decision-maker
**Focus:** Strategy · Accountability · Outcomes · Defensibility.
Assign when the item has implications for how policy is designed, how
accountability is structured, or how organisations defend decisions
to ministers, boards, or the public.

### Inia — Internal adviser
**Focus:** Evidence · Methods · Implementation · Sound decisions.
Assign when the item concerns how analysis is done, how evidence is
gathered or weighted, how implementation is structured, or how
decisions are made to be robust.

### Pete — Operational decision-maker
**Focus:** Clarity · Practicality · Reducing ambiguity.
Assign when the item concerns operational clarity, reducing
regulatory ambiguity, practical guidance for agencies, or how
implementation actually gets done on the ground.

---

## 8. Newsletter Insert Structure (Section 4)

Each insert follows this sequence:

1. **Title** *(required)* — Specific, number-driven, slightly
   unexpected. Max 12 words. Not a summary of the source; earn the
   click.
2. **Lead sentence** — The *"so what"* first. Don't bury the finding.
   No throat-clearing. One sentence.
3. **Context paragraph** — 2–3 sentences. What's changing and why it
   matters to NZ/AU policy. NZ/AU priority.
4. **Implication line** — One sentence. What does this mean for the
   reader's work or decisions?
5. **Persona tag + "So what for us" line** — Flag the persona this
   item serves most (*Debbie / Inia / Pete*). Follow with a short note
   on how this connects to Allen + Clarke's work or a client
   situation. Useful to the reader, not directed at them.

Total length: 100–150 words (body only, excluding title and source).
The compose agent counts and retries once if out of range.

## 9. Vocabulary

**Use these:**

- *practical*, *outcomes*, *results*, *impact*
- *evidence*, *evidence-based*
- *partners*, *partnership*
- *transform*, *transformation* (only where genuinely warranted)
- *communities*, *sectors*, *agencies*
- *advisers*, *consultants* (for the profession)

**Avoid:**

- *stakeholder* → prefer *affected parties*, *sector participants*, or
  the specific group by name
- *leverage*, *synergy*, *ecosystem*, *robust*, *unpack*, *deep dive*
- *regulatory instrument* → say *rules*, *regulations*, or the
  specific statute
- *going forward*, *at this point in time*, *in order to*
- Hedging clusters: *may potentially*, *could possibly*

---

## 10. Language Conventions

- **NZ/British English** spelling throughout.
- **Māori language:** Use macrons (*Māori, whānau, iwi, kaupapa*).
  Do not translate te reo terms inline unless context genuinely
  requires it.
- **Te Tiriti o Waitangi / Treaty of Waitangi:** Use *te Tiriti* when
  the Māori text is the specific referent; *Treaty of Waitangi* for
  the broader constitutional relationship. Do not abbreviate to *the
  Treaty* without prior full reference.
- **Dates in prose:** *8 April 2026.*
- **Dates in data:** ISO 8601 (*2026-04-08*).
- **Numbers:** Spell out one to nine; numerals for 10+. Always
  numerals for measurements, percentages, currencies, and submission
  counts.
- **Acronyms:** Full name on first reference with acronym in
  parentheses, e.g. *Ministry for the Environment (MfE)*. Acronym
  only thereafter within the same item.
- **Capitalisation:** *Government* (capital G) for the NZ or AU
  government as an institution; lowercase generic. *Ministry*
  capitalised only when naming a specific ministry.

---

## 11. Facts and News Only

The newsletter covers factual news only — government announcements,
regulator consultations, statutory body releases, law-firm summaries,
academic publications. Partisan content, opinion pieces, editorials,
election coverage, and advocacy material are rejected at triage and
never reach the brief.

In writing, this means:

- Report what happened. Do not adjudicate whether it should have.
- No adjectives that imply judgment (*overdue, welcome, troubling,
  long-awaited*).
- Where a regulation is contested, describe the dispute — name the
  parties, link the source — without endorsing a side.

Do not take sides, even by omission.

---

## 12. Prohibited Content

- Speculation beyond what the source directly states
- Client names (current or former)
- Partisan political commentary
- Predictions about election outcomes or government formation
- Hyperbole or promotional superlatives (*world-class, best-in-class,
  cutting-edge*)
- Emojis

---

## 13. Writing Craft — Making It Engaging

### The lead sentence does the work

The first sentence carries the finding, not the setup. Readers decide
in one sentence whether to keep going.

- **Weak:** *This week we look at some recent developments in the
  area of regulatory reform across the Australasian region.*
- **Strong:** *Australia's new algorithmic impact rules will reach
  NZ-based suppliers — and most aren't ready.*

### Specific beats general

- **Weak:** *The proposals will affect the regulatory landscape.*
- **Strong:** *The proposals shift five pricing decisions from the
  Commerce Commission to MBIE.*

### Titles

- **Weak:** *New AI regulation framework announced by government*
- **Strong:** *3 accountability gaps the NZ AI framework still
  hasn't closed*

### "So what for us" line

- **Weak:** *Contact us to find out more.*
- **Strong:** *This sits close to the work we've been doing with
  regulated entities on readiness assessments — worth flagging to
  anyone in that stream.*

### Concrete verbs

*requires → forces. facilitates → lets. enables → opens up.
impacts → changes, raises, cuts. addresses → name what it does.*

### Vary rhythm

Target: *medium, medium, short* or *long, short, medium.* A short
sentence after two long ones lands.

### Sentences to delete on sight

- *It is important to note that…*
- *It should be noted that…*
- *In recent times…*
- *Moving forward…*
- *There are a number of…*
- *With respect to…*

### Read it aloud

If a sentence is hard to say in one breath, split it. Cap: 28 words
unless splitting destroys the meaning.

## 14. Publishing Checklist

- [ ] NZ/British spelling throughout
- [ ] Lead sentence contains the *"so what"* in the first 10 words
- [ ] No paragraph longer than 4 sentences
- [ ] Title is specific and number-driven (not a summary)
- [ ] Title ≤ 12 words
- [ ] Bold used for key ideas only, not decoration
- [ ] Persona tag included
- [ ] *"So what for us"* line is named, low-pressure, and
      service-specific
- [ ] Data points are specific (named numbers/methodologies, not
      vague qualifiers)
- [ ] Insert body is 100–150 words
- [ ] Source URL included
